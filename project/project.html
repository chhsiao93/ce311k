Fluid Mechanics#

Bisection method#

The following figure shows a rectangular open channel of constant dimensions. Under uniform flow conditions, the flow in the channel is given by Manning’s equation:

\[Q = \frac{1}{n}\sqrt{S}\, A\, R^{2/3}\]

where \(S\) is the slope of the channel, \(A\) is the area of the channel, \(R\) is the hydraulic radius of the channel (the radius of a circle with equal area), and \(n\) is Manning’s roughness coefficient. For a rectangular channel, the following relationships hold:

\[A = By \qquad R = \frac{By}{B + 2y}\]

where \(y\) is the depth of the water in the channel.

Substitute the relationships above into Manning’s equation and determine the non-linear equation to be solved. Write a function to compute the depth of water \(y\) in the channel using the bisection method.

Using the following data: \(Q = 14.15\, \mathrm{m}^3/\mathrm{s}\), \(B = 4.572\,\mathrm{m}\), \(n = 0.017\), \(S = 0.0015\), and a suitable initial guess range of \(1, 2\), solve for the depth of water in the channel. Use a absolute tolerance of \(1 \times 10^{-6}\). Compute the number of iterations required to reach the solution and store your answer in n_iter
